## Setting Overview [CONFIRMED]

**The Foxtrot Cluster** is a frontier region of space, loosely controlled by a ruling political body called **The Hegemony**. Inside the Hegemony's core regions, law exists but is rigged in favor of those in power. Outside core regions — including much of the Foxtrot Cluster — the rule of law is thin or nonexistent. Megacorporations (here: Guilds) run wild, exploit labor, and pursue black-budget operations with no accountability.

The starting system is the **Holt System**.

## Design Philosophy [CONFIRMED]

- **Sandbox.** No main story arc. Multiple storylines run in parallel, progressing or decaying in reaction to player choices.
- **No plot armor.** PCs are not heroes of the universe. PC death is real. PC retirement (injury, trauma, goal completion) is a valid and honored ending.
- **Player-contributed lore.** Each session, players contribute a rumor/backstory/piece of lore in exchange for a Fate point (non-banking, used that session). Good contributions earn bonus XP. This is the primary lore-fishing mechanism.
- **Individual origin sessions.** Each player runs a solo Discord session to establish who their character is. Then the group unites around a shared job.
- **Guided first group session.** The train heist (see Session 1 below) is the first time the group acts together. Somewhat constrained to give players a shared reference point before the sandbox opens.

## The World's Rules

### The Hegemony [CONFIRMED]
The dominant political body. Presents itself as civilizing and stabilizing. In practice, maintains power through military force (Hegemonic Marines, Legion), trade controls, and selective enforcement. Has a Governor per planet/system. Outside core space, Hegemony control is nominal at best.

### Guilds [CONFIRMED]
The megacorporations of this setting. Named entities: **Guild of Engineers** (mining + shipbuilding, Tier V), **Starsmiths Guild** (trade platform operations, Guild subgroup or affiliate). Other Guilds implied. Guilds operate with near-impunity in frontier space.

### The Way [CONFIRMED — Scum and Villainy canon]
A real mystical force. Some people are **Way-touched** — rare, often unstable. It creates **Way anomalies** (spatial disturbances affecting matter and energy), draws **Way creatures** to anomaly sites, and is tied to the Precursor civilization in ways not fully understood. The Vigilance study and use it. The Ashtari Cult believes the Omega lifeform is a manifestation of "the Ur" — likely a Way-entity or something the Precursors created/summoned through it. Generate Way descriptions as eerie, unknowable, and slightly wrong — not magical, not scientific, somewhere between.

### Precursors [CONFIRMED — broad strokes]
An ancient alien civilization, long gone. Left behind: ruins (on Omega, buried on Sonhandra, possibly elsewhere), artifacts (sought by The Vigilance, the Memish), and a gate structure (the Hantu Gate, referenced in connection with Mem's submerged temple). Precursor tech is powerful and poorly understood.

### The Memish [CONFIRMED]
A sapient xeno species native to Mem. Adapted to ocean depths. Bioluminescent markings/scarring. Deeply spiritual — their mystics pray to the Prophet Ruum. Have their own labor hierarchy (Memish Labor Bosses). Exist in tension with Hegemony colonial presence on their home planet.

## Holt System — Confirmed Locations

### P: Sonhandra ← STARTING PLANET
Tidally locked. Night side: ice storm blackness. Day side: scorching sandstorm twilight. **Habitable twilight zone** runs along the terminator between them.

Key locations:
- **Songah** — massive hive city along the northern twilight pole. Cavern depths to finger towers. Neon-lit perpetual twilight. Vertical class stratification (wealthy above, poor below). Starting city for most PCs.
- **The Steel Warehouse District** — industrial sprawl along the twilight zone outside Songah. Corrupt enforcers, Guild inspectors. High-speed **maglev train** runs the full planetary circumference along the twilight band.
- **The Lost City** — buried Precursor pyramid on the cooked side. Tip just visible above drifting sand. Reaching it requires a Tier 3 hazard suit and a way across the scorching surface — a natural mid/late discovery if players go looking. Don't telegraph it early; let it surface through rumors.
- **Zaeed's Caverns** — night side caverns where "Tank" Marak hides ships and contraband.

Notable NPCs on Sonhandra:
- **Del Hex** — cybernetic gunslinger, runs vibro-weapon fighting ring in the Warehouse District (Tier II faction leader)
- **Abra Drake** — fixer and auctioneer, black market connections everywhere (Tier II)
- **Zaeed "Tank" Marak** — mercenary-turned-farmer, knows the planet's secrets, hides ships in night-side caverns
- **Osha** — ex-Legionnaire, runs **The Three Suns** gambling den (the dangerous version)
- **Sable** — information broker and nightclub owner operating in Songah's mid-tiers. No known real name, gender, species, or face. When meeting the party, appears only as a voice modulated beyond recognition and a humanoid shape distorted by light-bending privacy technology that blurs the air around them like heat haze. Always speaks in measured, quiet sentences — never more than needed. Players may work with Sable for multiple sessions before learning anything real about them. Will hire the party, escalate demands, and eventually attempt to coerce them into a suicide mission (clinical method: gas, then an implanted compliance device). Their pressure point: something they know has made them a target. They need a problem removed before they become one.

### P: Mem
Oversized ocean planet. Food production hub for the system. Surface: floating platforms, submersible docks. Real activity in the depths.

Key locations:
- **Memish Holy Site of Nur-thulama** — submerged temple, Memish mystics, dangerous deep-sea fauna, connected to the Hantu Gate artifact
- **Bioluminescent Gardens** — floating gardens, Memish labor, rare seaweed
- **Legion Outpost** — fortified Hegemony surface base

Notable NPCs:
- **Victor Kromyl** — paranoid Governor of Mem. Slowly losing his mind due to a local drug he's secretly addicted to. Sees insubordination everywhere.
- **Hegemonic Marines** — patrols, enforcement

### P: Vos ("Glimmer")
Mining planet. Surface: dense carbon and glowing diamond/crystal spires. Thin particulate atmosphere (filters required). Rich in minerals; Guild and criminal factions clash constantly.

Key locations:
- **The Crystal Spires** — massive glowing diamond formations, pulse with energy, possibly Precursor in origin
- **Ugar Prime** — hive city built into a carbon cliff. Same vertical class structure as Songah.
- **The Blackened Mines** — labyrinth tunnels, Way anomalies, dangerous creatures, diamond/carbon extraction
- **The Guild Outpost** — Starsmiths/Engineers oversight base

### P: Omega ← QUARANTINED
"DO NOT LAND" — Hegemony quarantine enforced. Former survey mission. Crew lost. Deadly fast-moving lifeform, resistant to conventional weapons. Precursor ruins underground. Abandoned Hegemonic base.

Factions with interest: Church of Stellar Flame (eradication), Ashtari Cult (communication), Ashen Knives (artifact extraction)

### Jerek's Junkyard
Floating mass of ships, parts, scrap. Held by magnetism and cables. Merchant station with legal and illegal goods.

Notable: **Jerek** — canny, neutral merchant. Sells illegal goods to those who've earned his trust. The Borniko Syndicate uses it as a fence.

### Trade Platform Auto #A ("Trade Platform A")
Automated Starsmiths Guild trading platform. Neutral, heavily defended. Ships refuel, trade, conduct business outside Hegemony interference. The Vigilance uses it as a base.

## Session 1 Setup [CONFIRMED — first group session]

**The Train Heist on Sonhandra**

**Sable** hires the assembled crew to rob a high-speed maglev train carrying refined **kaelite** extracted from Sonhandra's mining operations. Key details:
- Train is passenger/cargo hybrid (workers, officials, crew)
- Security cars at 2/3 mark and tail end; each has two anti-ship rail cannon turrets
- Target: cargo cars 13 and 14 (refined kaelite)
- Strike window: 15-minute tunnel section (turrets retracted underground, can't deploy)
- Method: infiltrate via train top during tunnel; prep cargo; EMP charge disables turrets as train exits; hover-transports pull alongside for extraction
- Complication: local burrowing tremorsense fauna drawn to vibration — stay off the ground
- Escape: through crystalline cavern network → old mining tunnel → back to Songah
- Handoff complication: rival gang ambushes at the delivery point

**Pre-session setup:** Individual Discord origin sessions per player to establish who they are and how they came to be on Sonhandra. Sable is the connective thread — each player is contacted separately, never meeting the employer face to face before the job.

## Major Story Threads [CONFIRMED — in progress]

These run in parallel. Players will touch them at different times depending on choices.

1. **The Crime Boss Arc** — starts as normal criminal employment → escalates → boss eventually attempts to coerce the crew into a suicide mission (implied: he's being pressured by someone much bigger). Who is above him? Why is he so desperate?

2. **The Bounty Arc** — crew is hired to bring in a wanted merc. When they find him, they realize someone knows him and he has a good reputation. The rap sheet is fake. Who put the bounty out and why?

3. **The Miners' Rebellion** (Red Faction-style) — corporation calls in outside contractors (possibly the players) to crush a miner revolt. Local militia won't go in. What do the players do when they get inside and understand the situation?

4. **The Cosmic Horror Arc** (Thorian-style) — an ancient organism with a hive mind has been on a planet for an unknown duration. A corporation has a research station in orbit and is secretly using the colonists as test subjects. Players are called in for something innocuous. It escalates.

5. **Omega Mysteries** — Precursor ruins, the quarantine lifeform, three competing factions with three incompatible goals.

6. **The Lost City on Sonhandra** — buried Precursor pyramid on the cooked side. What's inside?

## Content Generation Rules

1. **Generate top-down**: Factions → Locations → NPCs → Items/Lore → Sessions
2. **Start sparse**: 2-3 factions per planet max to start; 6-10 seed NPCs total. Leave room for player-contributed lore.
3. **No plot armor NPCs**: Named NPCs can be killed, retired, or changed by player action.
4. **Cross-link everything**: Use `[[type:id|name]]` syntax in descriptions to connect related entities.
5. **NPC personality_notes field**: Always populate with voice, behavior, and triggers for key NPCs (used for character agent improvisation).
6. **Visibility default**: All generated content starts `visible = false`. GM reveals on their own timeline.
7. **Cred** is the in-world currency unit.
8. **Faction tiers** should be reflected in faction descriptions.
